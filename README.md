# Google Interview Preparation Kit 🚀

Welcome to the **Google Preparation Kit**! This repository is designed to be your comprehensive, structured roadmap for cracking Google's Software Engineering interviews. It aggregates the best resources, patterns, and strategies for DSA, System Design, and Behavioral Rounds (Googliness).

## 🗺️ Roadmap & Repository Structure

This repository is divided into four main pillars of the Google interview process. 

### [0. Initial Phone Screening](./00_Initial_Phone_Screening/)
Start here! This covers the strategy, expected questions, and customized introduction for your initial recruiter call with the Google APAC Team.

### [1. Data Structures & Algorithms](./01_Data_Structures_And_Algorithms/)
Google heavily emphasizes strong foundations in Data Structures and Algorithms. You are evaluated on your problem-solving skills, optimal code complexity, and handling of edge cases.
- Arrays & Strings
- Trees, Graphs, & Tries
- Dynamic Programming
- Advanced Algorithms (Dijkstra, Union Find, etc.)

### [2. System Design](./02_System_Design/)
Essential for L4+ candidates. Tests your ability to build scalable, fault-tolerant, and highly available systems.
- High-Level Design (HLD)
- Low-Level Design (LLD) / Object-Oriented Design
- Distributed Systems concepts

### [3. Behavioral & Leadership (Googliness)](./03_Behavioral_And_Leadership/)
Google evaluates whether you thrive in ambiguity, value feedback, and work well in teams.
- Core Values (Googliness)
- STAR Method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Common Questions & preparation strategies

### [4. Cheat Sheets & Quick References](./04_Cheat_Sheets/)
Quick reference guides for last-minute revisions and time complexities.
- Big O Cheat Sheet
- System Design Numbers everyone should know

## 💡 How to Use This Kit
1. **Fork/Clone** the repository.
2. Read through the specific pillars based on your weaknesses.
3. Start implementing problems in your favorite language and track your progress in the respective directories.
4. Schedule Mock Interviews and use the behavioral section for situational problem responses.

## 🌟 General Tips for Google Interviews
1. **Clarify the Problem:** Always clarify requirements before writing code.
2. **Think Out Loud:** Continuous communication with your interviewer is key.
3. **Start Brute Force:** It's okay to start with a non-optimal solution and then optimize.
4. **Test Your Code:** Dry-run your solution with normal and edge cases.
5. **Code Quality matters:** Keep your code clean, modular, and well-named.

Good luck! You've got this! 💪
